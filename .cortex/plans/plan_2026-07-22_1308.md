## Context

**Data available in Snowflake (`MET_SCRATCH`):**
- `THAMES.RIVER_THAMES` / `RIVER_THAMES_CONNECTED` — Thames river geometry as GEOGRAPHY LineStrings (~527 links)
- `THAMES.STATIONS` — 34 EA stations with lat/long (temperature stations have `LABEL ILIKE '%THAMES%'`, not RIVER_NAME)
- `THAMES.CATCHMENT_BOUNDARIES` — 671 polygons as GEOGRAPHY, joinable via NRFA_STATION_ID
- `THAMES.READINGS` — 3.3M rows, parameters: flow, dissolved oxygen, temperature, level, conductivity, etc.
- `CLIMATE.THAMES_TEMPERATURE_TIMESERIES` — 15.6M rows hourly air temp, 116 sites, 2011–2026
- `CLIMATE.THAMES_PRECIPITATION_TIMESERIES` — matching hourly precip
- `CLIMATE.THAMES_WATER_TEMPERATURE_DAILY` — derived daily water temp, 2021–2026 (1,825 rows)
- `CLIMATE.THAMES_WATER_TEMPERATURE_FORECAST` — 7-day forecast
- `FISH.THAMES_10` — 10 species with thermal tolerance bands
- `FISH.THAMES_FISH_POPULATION_SIMULATION` — 54,750 rows (day × species × scenario)
- `FISH.THAMES_FISH_POPULATION_SUMMARY` — 30 rows final outcomes

**Deployment:** Streamlit in Snowflake, container runtime, PyPI EAI for `folium`/`streamlit-folium`/`plotly`.

**Key constraint:** `st.pydeck_chart` and `st.map` work natively. Folium needs PyPI access via container runtime. Geography data is extractable via `ST_ASGEOJSON()`.

### Step 2: `streamlit/data.py` — Snowflake queries (cached)

- `get_connection()` — uses `st.connection("snowflake")` (native in SiS)
- `get_stations()` — returns DataFrame of all stations with lat/long
- `get_river_geojson()` — `SELECT ST_ASGEOJSON(GEOMETRY) FROM RIVER_THAMES_CONNECTED` → FeatureCollection
- `get_catchment_geojson(nrfa_id)` — single catchment polygon as GeoJSON
- `get_station_readings(station_id, parameter)` — timeseries for a station
- `get_air_temp_timeseries(site_id, start, end)` — hourly from CLIMATE
- `get_water_temp_daily()` — daily water temp 2021–2026
- `get_water_temp_forecast()` — 7-day forecast
- `get_fish_species()` — THAMES_10 thermal bands
- `get_population_simulation(species, scenario)` — daily sim data

All wrapped with `@st.cache_data(ttl=600)`.

### Step 3: `pages/1_The_Challenge.py` — User Journey

- Persona card: EA Fisheries Officer / Thames Environment Manager
- Two-column layout: **Current state** (manual checks, fragmented data, reactive) vs **Future state** (integrated dashboard, early warning, proactive)
- Brief text explaining the challenge statement
- Static — no heavy queries

### Step 4: `pages/2_The_River.py` — Interactive Map

- Full-width folium map centred on the Thames (~51.5, -1.0, zoom 9)
- **River layer**: GeoJSON LineStrings from `RIVER_THAMES_CONNECTED` (blue lines)
- **Station markers**: CircleMarkers for each station (colour-coded by available parameters)
- **Interaction**: `st.selectbox` to pick a station → updates:
  - Catchment polygon overlay (if NRFA_STATION_ID exists)
  - Sidebar/expander with station metadata
  - Line chart of readings (parameter selectable: temperature, flow, level, DO)
- Use `st_folium(map, returned_objects=["last_object_clicked"])` for click → station selection

### Step 5: `pages/3_Climate_Signal.py` — Temperature Trends

- **Site selector**: pick a Met Office site (or "Thames average")
- **Date range**: `st.date_input` for start/end
- **Hourly air temperature plot** (plotly line chart, with daily rolling mean overlay)
- **Daily water temperature** line chart (2021–2026) from `THAMES_WATER_TEMPERATURE_DAILY`
- **Correlation scatter**: air temp vs water temp (daily aggregates)
- **Summer stress hours by year**: bar chart of hours above 26°C / 30°C / 33°C

### Step 6: `pages/4_Fish_at_Risk.py` — Forecast & Population Model

- **7-day forecast panel**: water temp forecast with horizontal colour bands for each species' optimum/stress/lethal zones (plotly figure with `add_hrect`)
- **Species selector** + **scenario toggle** (baseline / +3°C / +5°C)
- **Population simulation chart**: line plot of population over 365 simulated days, with thermal deaths annotated
- **Summary table**: `st.dataframe` with conditional formatting showing final pop, deaths, % change per species per scenario

### Step 7: `streamlit_app.py` — Navigation entrypoint

```python
import streamlit as st

pg = st.navigation([
    st.Page("pages/1_The_Challenge.py", title="The Challenge", icon="🎯"),
    st.Page("pages/2_The_River.py", title="The River", icon="🗺️"),
    st.Page("pages/3_Climate_Signal.py", title="Climate Signal", icon="🌡️"),
    st.Page("pages/4_Fish_at_Risk.py", title="Fish at Risk", icon="🐟"),
])
st.set_page_config(page_title="Fried Fish 🐟🔥", layout="wide")
pg.run()
```
