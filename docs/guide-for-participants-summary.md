# Guide for Participants — Summary

Source: `docs/pdfs/Guide-for-participants-Improving-the-Use-of-Weather-and-Climate-Data-for-Smarter-Decision-Making.pdf`

## Purpose

A structured methodology to take teams from **challenge → solution** over the course of the hackathon. It's a toolkit, not a checklist — use whatever questions/templates help your team think; you don't need to complete every activity. Grounded in a **human-centred design** approach: understand real users and their decisions before designing a solution.

## The Methodology (7 steps)

1. **Define Your Challenge & Explore Data**
   - Pick a theme, agree a challenge statement in the form: *"How might we ___ so that ___ can ___?"*
   - Explore available datasets: what's available, what insights/trends they offer, what decisions they could inform, who benefits, and what initial solution ideas emerge.

2. **Stakeholder Mapping**
   - Identify who is directly/indirectly affected, who decides, who benefits, who could be negatively impacted.
   - Plot on an impact/involvement map (High impact & direct user → Medium → Low).

3. **Define Your User Persona**
   - Build empathy for one real user: role & organisation, current tools/ways of working, challenges/pain points, and the future value your solution could bring them.
   - Example given: **Sarah Flakes**, Senior Urban Planner at "Combined Authority X" — needs trusted, integrated climate + housing data to pick resilient development sites.

4. **Use Case & User Journey**
   - Part A (current state): map the user's existing steps, data/tools used, pain points, and decisions made — *without* your solution.
   - Part B (future state): map the same journey *with* your solution — inputs, outputs, decisions supported, and benefits realised.

5. **Prototyping & User Validation**
   - Define the core capability, what problem each feature solves, what data is needed, and what outputs are generated.
   - Build a wireframe/storyboard and develop the prototype using **Snowflake** and other available tools.
   - Optional: validate with the user/another participant — what worked, what confused, what's missing.

6. **Roadmap & Next Steps**
   - Show how the idea could progress beyond the hackathon: assumptions to test, data/technical work still needed, who needs to be involved, risks/barriers, and how success would be measured.

7. **Slide Template**
   - 5 slides per team, minimum 2 slides showing the solution/idea itself.

## Demonstrations, Presentations & Judging

- Each team presents for a **maximum of 5 minutes**.
- Judged by a panel against three weighted criteria (rated 1–5 each):

| Criterion | Weight | What it assesses |
|---|---|---|
| **Technology & Innovation** | 50% | Originality, creativity, improvement over existing Met Office services; strength of approach; potential to increase data use/access |
| **Impact & Value** | 30% | How well the challenge/use case is addressed; benefit created for users, organisations, or society |
| **Feasibility** | 20% | Practicality of implementing into existing systems, including ongoing operation and maintenance |

- The **winning idea may be considered by the Met Office for advancement into alpha-stage production** — this is a real path to impact, not just a demo.
